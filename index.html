Sell-side valuation · welding technology / mechanical engineering · 90 minutes

Task

You are an M&A advisor at WINTERGERST. The shareholder of LORCH Welding Technology GmbH (Auenwald, BW) has tasked you with preparing a sale process. Your tasks:

  • Derive adjusted EBITDA from raw data (EBITDA Bridge)
  • Select a peer group from publicly listed comparable companies
  • Analyze and filter 10–12 real precedent transactions
  • Assess working capital adjustment
  • Calculate enterprise value and equity value
  • Create a football field chart and sensitivity matrix
Important note: In Modules 3 and 4, there are intentionally unsuitable peers and transactions. Filtering them out and justifying your decisions is a core part of the task.
Key terms (quick glossary): HGB = German accounting standard; EUR k = thousand euros; NWC = net working capital (receivables + inventory - payables); EV = enterprise value; Equity Bridge = EV to equity value reconciliation (debt/cash/NWC); DVFA = German valuation guidance often used for management adjustments; LTM = last twelve months / NTM = next twelve months (EBITDA basis).

Module 1 · Company Profile

LORCH Welding Technology GmbH – Company Overview

Business Model

Premium manufacturer of welding power sources (MIG-MAG, TIG, stick electrode, automation/cobot) under the brand "smart welding." Headquarters: Auenwald, Baden-Württemberg. Production entirely in Germany (build-to-order via SAP). Sales in >60 countries, >65% export share, 7 own foreign subsidiaries.

Founded in 1957, family-owned business (owner-managed), ~280 FTE worldwide, >50 in R&D. 25,000 welding machines/year. Digitalization push: Lorch Connect (IoT/remote monitoring), cobot welding (partnership with Yaskawa since 2020).

Core risk: In FY 2022, Russia sanctions led to an EBITDA impact of EUR k 172 (booked as "Change in Other Operating Expenses"). Working capital was inflated by elevated order backlog (peak EUR k 8,203 in May 2022 vs. normal EUR k 2,500).

Discount considerations

Assess: which systematic differences between your selected peers and LORCH require a discount to the observed peer multiple?

Notes on peer selection:

Include: LECO (17,7x) and ESAB (11,5x) – direct product overlap, best available public comps. Kemppi (12.0x LTM est.) – structurally the closest analogue to LORCH, but with methodological limitations (not publicly listed).

Exclude:
ITW (17,8x): The EV/EBITDA relates to the group. The welding segment is ~1/7 of the group. A conglomerate multiple includes diversification premiums, financing structure, and segment mix. No segment-specific EV/EBITDA can be derived → methodologically invalid.
Cognex (38,5x): Machine vision systems, no welding power sources. SaaS/IP model with recurring-revenue premium → multiple based on a fundamentally different business model.
Watts Water (17,2x): Water regulation technology. No product overlap, different end market, different demand profile.
Norsk Hydro (8,4x): Commodity/material group with cyclical profile. Low EBITDA margin (12%) due to capital-intensive metal production. Wrong sector and wrong size segment.

Discount logic: peer median ~14x → LORCH target range 9–13x after a 20–25% haircut for size/liquidity and margin gap.
